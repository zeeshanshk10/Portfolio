Overview

Music uplifts your state of mind. There is right kind of music for every mood of ours. The variety of Music which we are offered to us is kind of exciting and overwhelming at the same time. It is important that we, as users, get the right Music player app which understands our taste and suggests the right kind of music from the vast ocean of songs, genres, artists and more.

"Music is the moonlight in the gloomy night of life". - Paul Friedrich Richter

Challenges

There were 3 key goals that I had to accomplish for this project:

1. To design a UI which is creative but also familiar to the users.
2. The interface should be easy to use and navigate and therefore it should be intuitive and consistent.
3. To create a UI which is attractive and at the same time comfortable to interact with.

Style Guide


I wanted to go with the dark theme for the UI because, as per user research music apps are used by the majority of tenegers during night time. Dark mode is sleek, cool, and elegant, which could be why nearly 85% of people prefer to use it, so for the color palette, I opted for dark shades with btight color gradients.

User Research


I adopted one of the primary methods for conducting the user research- user surveys, with a survey size of 100. The motive behind selecting research method was to get a quantitative data to identify the user tastes, behavior and preferences. The survey form which I prepared covered a range of questions. Some of the relevant questions are listed below:


1. Which apps do you use to listen to music?

2. Do you like to explore new music or are you happy listening to your regular playlist?

3. How do you discover new music?

4. Do you listen to suggested songs from a particular music app?

5. Do you create your own playlist?

6. How often do you share music with others?

7. Do you prefer the Dark or Light theme in our music player app?

Wireframes

After empathizing with users, I created the wireframes. I always keep my initial wireframes simple, in order to be able to quickly test them with users early in the process. Next, I digitalized my wireframes in Figma.


User Testing

Using my professional network, I recruited 5 participants to accomplish a task scenario. I wanted to ensure that the flow and layout of my wireframes were intuitive and simple before moving on to finalizing my design.


Changes in Design after User Testing

Taking into considerations all of the above faults in my old design, I recreated the new design with the necessary changes, added new features, animations, micro interactions and made it more desirable and efficient to use.


User Feedback:
